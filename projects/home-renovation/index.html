Garage

  • Note about garage fire ‘separationj’fire separation
  • Drywall ceiling in the garage needs to be 5/8” Type “X”
  • So this means no return air registers or heat supply registers inside the garage.
  • garage door

To meet fire separation requirements, the door must be one of three types of doors:  a solid wood door not less than 1-3/8 inches thick, a solid or honeycomb core steel doors not less than 1-3/8 inches thick, or a 20->minute fire-rated door, with a self-closing device.  Another thing to note, is that a 1-3/4″ thick solid wood raised panel door would likely not comply because the minimum thickness at the recesses would likely be less >than 1-3/8.”

Wood Floors

General Info

Gaps in Floors

The gaps in the floors are due to moistures that change with the season

  • gaps usually occur during the summer when the moisture is high
  • it will start closing up during drier period
  • therefore it is not recommended to fill floor cracks with fillers

Sanding Notes

General Notes for Sanding

  • start the machine before setting it down
  • start from the left of the room
  • work half the room first, the the other haft
  • go with the grain, and lift when finishing
    • follow the same path back
    • overlap the next path by about 4 inches
  • 140sqft will take average about 5 hours

Prepping for Sanding

  • Use 2 in countersunk trim screw to fix all the squeaks first before sanding

Finishing Notes

  • Make sure windows are open to about 3”” to allow fresh air to come in
  • turn on fan to circulate air
  • Make sure the temp of the rooms are above 70 degrees
  • Start from the farthest corner from the the exit door
  • Make sure to overlap
  • Feather at all turns, stops, and starts

Prepping to apply Finish

  • Vacuum the floor and wipe down with tack cloth
    • Use an old t-shirt or microfiber cloth
    • Coat cloth with paint thinner and pick up all dust and to reveal finish
  • Remove all lint from the applicator!

Finish - Waterlox

  • not recommended to take on wood filler
    • Due to contracting and expansion of wood, this will work the filler loose between boards
  • If filler is used, make sure it is ““sandable”” and ““stainable””
  • 500 sqft per gallon
  • the floor will look uneven up to the third coat!
  • tack clean the floor before each coat!

Clean up

  • Waterlox can be cleaned with paint thinner
